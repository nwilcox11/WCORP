**Opening Call**
**Indicative Opening Price**
- Large institutions place orders on the book prior to the open. 
	- Based on these orders sitting on the book the exchange calculates an opening price **(IOP)**. 
- We can gain some sentiment around direction based on **Open Price** and if participants think its fair value or not.

**The Open**
- First half hour of trade tends to **establish one of the day's extremes**.
- We can use the first few minutes to try to establish which extreme will hold.
- Types of Opens
	1) Open-Drive
	2) Open-Test-Drive
	3) Open-Rejection-Reverse
	4) Open-Auction

**Context**
- Observe by reviewing **Globex** activity.
- Cash open print.
- Early volume/price action 15-60 mins.

**Open-Drive**
- Strongest and most definitive type of open.
- Caused by OTF participants who have made their market decisions **before** the opening bell. 

- Market opens **above or below** the previous days value area.
- **Aggressive auctions in one direction**
	- Price never returns to trade back through opening range in early minutes.
- **High OTF Initiative activity**

**Bullish Open-Drive**
- Market opens **above** previous value area/range - drives higher.
- The **open** often becomes (or is near) the **low of day**.

**Bearish Open-Drive
- Market opens **below** previous value area/range - drives lower
- The **open** becomes (or is near) the **high of day**.

**Open-Test-Drive**
- Lacks initial confidence necessary to drive immediately after the opening bell.
- Market generally opens and **tests beyond a known reference point (PDH/L, bracket top/bottom)**
	- We then get a reversal back through the open.
	- We get a **failed initial test** followed by a drive in the opposite direction.
		- The test below actually discouraged activity, buyers could then probe to the upside with conviction.
	- This often establishes one of the day's extremes.
- **Break to Rally** or **Rally to Break**

**Open-Rejection-Reverse**
- Market opens, trades in one direction, then meets opposite activity strong enough to reverse price and return it back through the opening range.
- An initial extreme is established when buying or selling in one direction dies out, auction stalls.
- Lower directional conviction tends to mean this will be a **two-sided** trading day.
- The key to performing well on a day like this is **_patience_**.
	- The day may have strong periods that look like breakouts, but the follow-through will not be there.

**Open-Auction**
- Market appears to open and randomly auction above and below the opening range.
- We becomes important is where the market **opened**
	- **Inside Previous day Range**
	- **Outside Previous day Range**

**Open-Auction in Range**
- Lower directional conviction, **unchanged sentiment**
- **Non-trend, normal, neutral** day.
- Look to let market establish extremes and perceived value area, when can then try and fade the extremes.

**Open-Auction out of Range**
- Since the market has opened **out of balance** we can assume OTF sentiment has changed and price will seek a higher or lower level.
- There is potential for this to be a bigger day.
