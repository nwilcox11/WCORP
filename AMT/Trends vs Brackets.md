**Trending Markets**
- Started by **initiated** action from the OTF. Price is perceived to be away from value.
- If a series of value area placements are moving in a clear direction through time, then new price levels are being accepted and the trend is continuing.
- As price auctions **higher/lower** the trend draws in market participants from many other timeframes until everyone is a **buyer/seller**.
- The trend comes to an end when the **buyer/seller** has **bought/sold**.
- When value areas begin to **overlap** or move in the **opposite direction** to the current trend trend is slowing and beginning to **balance**.
	- A trend will generally not come to an abrupt end and reverse. **There is usually a balancing period, where the trend could reverse or continue**.

**Bracketed Markets**
- Both **OTF buyer/seller** become **responsive parties**.
- As price nears the **top** of the bracket the seller responds and auctions price downward through the bracket. Buyer does the same at the **bottom**.
